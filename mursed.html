MURSED

Smart sewer monitoring using fiber-optic sensing and AI to detect leaks and blockages.

Full-Stack + Sensing Frontend: JS • Backend: Python
Background & Problem

Urban sewer networks suffer from leaks and blockages that can damage infrastructure and create environmental/health hazards. Conventional monitoring is periodic and reactive, so anomalies are discovered late and repairs are costly.

  • High cost from emergency repairs and collateral damage.
  • Public health risk due to leaks and potential toxic gas exposure.
  • Poor visibility across long pipes; limited ability to pinpoint issues quickly.
Proposed Solution & Aims

MURSED pairs fiber-optic sensing with AI-based anomaly detection to continuously monitor pipelines, detect/locate events (leaks, blockages), and support proactive maintenance via a web dashboard.

  • Sustainability: reduce contamination/overflows.
  • Accuracy & Efficiency: quicker, more precise localization → faster fixes.
  • Economic value: shift from reactive to predictive maintenance.
Data & Sensing Approach

Fiber-optic network along the pipelines captures signals (vibration, strain, acoustic signatures). The backend ingests streams, the AI detects patterns consistent with ruptures or clogs, and the app estimates their location along the line.

  • Continuous, long-distance monitoring suited for municipal scale.
  • Event detection + localization to guide field teams.
  • Dashboard concepts: anomaly list with priority, map view, auto-generated repair plan.
